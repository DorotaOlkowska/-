Pet shop

The first of my tasks in the creation of this pet shop was to come up with a concept for the name of the shop. As you can see, creativity has many sides :) I wanted the name of the shop to be out of the box and at the same time close to each customer. "Good Gucio!" is a reference to the name of the owner's dog and an oft-spoken phrase when the pet is polite.
The next step was to design a logo. The logo consists of the name of the shop and a signet created from a photography of the owner's dog. The unconventional shop name is combined with the striking colour of 100% magenta, which is intended to be an eye-catcher. It is worth noting that, depending on the form and colour of the graphic message, it is possible to change the 100% magenta colour in the logotype to another leading colour of the project in which the logo is used. This ensures that the logo still retains its character while avoiding the possibility of disrupting the colour scheme of a project.

Project Overview

Goal: Create a travel application that will contain, as far as possible, all the solutions you need throughout the entire travel process.
My role: UX Research, Information Architecture, UX Design

F I R S T L Y - D I S C O V E R

RESEARCH

My first step was to do some research. I analyzed the main, popular travel applications. I focused on getting to know the potential client and conducting interviews. I learned the main problems people encounter when using travel applications.

Feature Comparison Chart

The Feature Comparison Chart was created to compare application features. Thanks to this, I could easily see trends in the functionalities of various applications.
This helped me understand the diversity of travel apps and the fact that there is a lack of more comprehensive apps in the market.

After the above analysis, it can be concluded that Booking has the largest number of options. Unfortunately, it cannot boast of many attractions. In turn, Trip Advisor beats the competition in terms of providing attractions to travelers. During this research, I noticed that there was no application that would allow for a comprehensive journey.

Lean UX Canvas

In this part, I focused on the problem I want to solve and for whom. Lean UX Canvas is a tool that allows you to understand the business part and the user part.

This activity helped me organize the information I collected during the research. Thanks to this, I managed to create a general outline of this travel application.

IDI (ang. Individual In-Depth Interview)

IDI (Individual In-depth Interview) is a direct conversation between the researcher and the respondent. Every question I asked received a comprehensive answer and helped me create a potential recipient of the application in the next steps. Such an interview is structured (scenario) and has goals (getting answers to questions) that the researcher wants to achieve. This is a basic, qualitative research method, sometimes called field reconnaissance.

The interview consisted of 16 questions about experiences, pros and cons of the travel applications used, types of holidays chosen and the entire trip planning process from start to finish. I was interviewed by 5 people, at different stages of life. Thanks to this, I received potential audience profiles for my travel application. These interviews allowed me to move on to the next step, which was to create a persona.

S E C O N D L Y - D E F I N E

MAKING SENSE OF THE DATA

Niching Down: Persona Empathy Map

After these interviews I was able to make Persona Emapthy Map. I created two extreme personas based on interviews to expand the possibilities of customizing the application.

The first person is Laura. This is a young person at the beginning of her career path, she has no obligations in life. When traveling, price and freedom are important to her. She would like to be able to cancel trips without additional costs. She travels with friends and chooses active ways of spending time. She would like to app to help her plan her trip based on her preferences.

The second person is Agata. She is a 45-year-old woman with her husband, two children and a dog. She has a high social position and high earnings. During the holidays, maximum comfort is important to her, price is not that much important. She travels mainly on all-inclusive and spends her leisure time passively. She always travel with her dog, but unfortunately it makes traveling difficult. She could try traveling on her own, but for now it's too much effort for her due to the multitude of applications, all-inclusive offers are easier.

Customer Journey Map

At this stage, I focused on presenting the process that the user goes through when planning the travel.

The first customer map refers to a young, single person with a limited budget. We see a lot of use of applications to recommend attractions and compare prices to find the cheapest option.

The second customer map refers to a person with a large budget, family and stability. We see additional options here, such as the desire to purchase additional insurance and looking for places convenient for animals.

T H I R D L Y - D E V E L O P

PROBLEM MEETS SOLUTION

MVP (Minimum Viable Product)

Now, I was able to define the hierarchy of capabilities of the MVP version of the application.

As you can see, the most important options to include in MVP are: a personal questionnaire thanks to which travel options would be best suited to the user, the ability to store reservations, book accommodation from the application level and convenient payments. In addition, it seems that it would also be important to include the possibility of planning flights and events in the test version. Due to the initial version of the application, the ability to plan a trip from A to Z would have to be abandoned due to the high initial costs.

User Flow

The last thing before prototyping in my project is creating a User Flow. Thanks to that, I can plan and think about the operation of the application at the user level.

Due to the fact that the application is comprehensive, its operation has been described in detail in User Flow. The application starts with a login or registration screen, so to use the application you need to create an account here. This solution is intended to give the user the impression of belonging to a community. Moreover, having an account will mean possible discounts and rewards in the future.

Then, after positive verification, you will be able to choose a path, i.e. Taxi, Car Sharing, Events, Accommodation, Flights, My Reservations, Saved. Depending on the path chosen, the user will be guided through the entire process up to the purchase/reservation in the application. Each path will include a detailed questionnaire so that the results of searching will be tailored to the user as much as possible. The questionnaire will have a short survey containing key information such as price, quality, type of holiday, time, number of travelers, etc.

It is worth paying attention to the "Saved" category, which includes the ability not only to save selected offers for later, but also to share the offer in the application with other users. This is useful when one person is organizing a trip and wants to show the options to the rest of the participants. Moreover, "Saved" will allow you to divide selected options into categories, which can make easier complex travel planning. Additionally, it will be possible to manage reservations, even past ones - it will be simply to renew them. At this level, the application seems to meet user expectations, with the possibility of further development based on feedback.
